What Roadrunner does

Roadrunner adds the birds you logged on eBird to the Strava activity you logged them during. When a Strava activity's time window overlaps an eBird checklist, your activity description gets a tidy list of the species and counts from that checklist.

  1. It reads your most recent Strava activities and recent eBird checklists.
  2. It matches them by overlapping date and time.
  3. For each match, it writes a “Birds seen during activity” block into the Strava description — existing text is kept, and re-syncing never duplicates it.

Heads up: only eBird checklists with a recorded duration can be matched (incidental observations without a duration are skipped). Save your eBird checklist before your Strava activity uploads — if the activity arrives first, there's no checklist yet to match, so it won't be updated automatically (use Sync now after saving the checklist to catch it). New activities are otherwise picked up automatically a short time after they upload.
